Pho Recipe

Pho is a traditional Vietnamese noodle soup consisting of broth, rice noodles, herbs, and meat, usually beef or chicken. Here is a simple recipe to make delicious pho at home.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound beef bones (or chicken bones)
  • 8 cups water
  • 1 onion, halved
  • 3-inch piece of ginger, sliced
  • 2 star anise pods
  • 4 cloves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 8 ounces rice noodles (banh pho)
  • 1/2 pound thinly sliced beef (sirloin or brisket) or cooked chicken
  • Fresh herbs (cilantro, basil, mint)
  • Bean sprouts
  • Lime wedges
  • Sliced jalapeños or chili peppers
  • Sliced green onions

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, add beef bones and cover with water. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 10 minutes. Discard the water and rinse the bones to remove impurities.
  2. Refill the pot with 8 cups of fresh water. Add the onion, ginger, star anise, cloves, and cinnamon stick. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for at least 3 hours (the longer, the better for flavor).
  3. Strain the broth through a fine-mesh sieve into another pot. Discard the solids. Add fish sauce and sugar to the broth. Adjust seasoning to taste.
  4. Cook the rice noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  5. To serve, divide the cooked noodles among bowls. Top with thinly sliced beef or cooked chicken. Ladle hot broth over the meat to cook it.
  6. Garnish with fresh herbs, bean sprouts, lime wedges, sliced jalapeños, and green onions. Serve immediately.
